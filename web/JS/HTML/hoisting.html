üìú Hoisting in JavaScript

Hoisting is the behavior where function and variable declarations are moved to the top of their scope during the memory allocation phase.

  • Functions: Entire function is hoisted and available throughout the scope.
  • var Variables: Hoisted but initialized as undefined.
  • let / const: Placed in the TDZ until the line where they are declared.
